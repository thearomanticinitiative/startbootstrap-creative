Emergency Resources

Immediate Danger

If you or someone you know are in immediate danger, please call 911.

Suicide Prevention

The Trevor Project - 1-866-488-7386

The Trevor Project also has a live chatroom or text options avaliable in order to help you. They are avaliable 24/7.

National Suicide Prevention Lifeline-1-800-273-8255

The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline is a national network of local crisis centers that provides free and confidential emotional support to people in suicidal crisis or emotional distress 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Homelessness

Ali Forney Center

Provides housing programs and other resources to LGBT+ youth. Link includes more shelters for Arkansas, California, Colorado, Florida, Georgia, Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Maine, Massachusetts, Michigan, Minnesota, New Jersey, New York, North Carolina, Oklahoma, Oregon, Pennsylvania, Texas, Utah, Vermont, Washington, and Wisconsin. You can also find resources for LGBT+ families and ways to volunteer/help on their site.

National Runaway Safeline

They have a 24/7 phoneline, a live chat, email, and a forum. They are LGBT+ friendly and all information will remain confidential.

National Coalition for the Homeless

The National Coalition for Homelessness has resources for homeless or soon to be homeless people, as well as advocacy programs and ways you can help. They are LGBT+ friendly.

LGBT+ Shelters List

Lambda Legal, a LGBT+ advocacy group put together this list of resources by state. Note this is mostly for the major cities of each state. You can find their main site here if you are in search of legal help.

LGBT+ Center Directory

A directory of LGBT+ centers, including an interactive & searchable map with centers throughout the US as well as 3 internationally. You can add centers here.

LGBT+ Financial Aid

Financial aid provided by LGBT Support.

Abuse, Sexual Assault, and Violence

RAINN/National Sexual Assault Hotline

Hotline: 1-800-656-4673, or use their online chat service. Avaliable 24/7.

New York City Anti-Violence Project

Report violence you experience or witness to their free and confidential 24-hour bilingual (English/Spanish) hotline at 212-714-1141 where you can speak with a trained counselor and seek support, or you can report violence anonymously online, or to ask for a counselor to reach out to you.

FORGE

Serves transgender and gender non-conforming surviviors of domestic and sexual violence, provides referrals to local counselers.

The Network La Red

Hotline- 617-742-4911. Bilingual, supports LGBTQ, polyamourous, and kink/BDSM survivors.

The Northwest Network

Hotline- 206-568-7777. Serves LGBT survivors of abuse; can provide local referrals.
