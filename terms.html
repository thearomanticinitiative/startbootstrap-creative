Aro Community Terminology

Commonly used words and phrases in the aromantic community.

General

  • Alloromantic/Zedromantic/Zromantic-describes someone who is not aromantic spectrum

  • Allosexual/Zedsexual/Zsexual-describes someone who is not asexual spectrum. Aromantic people may be allosexual or asexual spectrum.

  • Asexual-feeling rare to no sexual attraction, or feeling sexual attraction only under certain circumstances.

  • Aro-short for aromantic

  • Arospec-short for the aromantic spectrum, which includes all identites that are not alloromantic.

  • Amatonormativity-social force where romantic relationships are treated as intrinsically better and more valuable than other sorts of relationships, and that forming such an exclusive, central, amororous relationship is a goal shared universally.

  • Romantic Orientation-describes to what gender(s), if any, a person experiences romantic attraction toward (romantic version of sexual orientation).

Describing Attraction

  • Alterous-experiences attraction that can only be described as a desire for emotional closeness because neither romantic or platonic attraction is accurate, is intended to be used in place of romantic or platonic. for example someone may identify as bi-alterous, poly-alterous, homo-alterous, etc. A person who identifies this way may experience varying degrees of attraction that is identifiable as romantic or platonic, but may feel discomfort, unease, or a sense of inaccuracy in calling it wholly romantic or platonic.

  • Plush-a “queerplatonic crush”, describes a strong qp attraction toward a person or wanting to form a qpr with someone.

  • Quasiromantic-describes someone whose attraction is seen as non-traditional or who may feel it differs from crushes, perhaps a mix between platonic, romantic, aesthetic, or somewhere completely different and/or it involves other non-traditional aspects, such as rare attraction, or attraction but non-physical, non-platonic but romantic, etc.

  • Romantic Attraction-romantic feelings or desires toward someone; often described as wishing to do romantic activities with them, also felt as “butterflies in the stomach” and “warm cozy feelings in the chest”

  • Squish-a “friend crush”, describes a strong platonic attraction toward a person. Can be used as a verb, similarly to crush.

  • Swish-an “aesthetic crush”, describes an aesthetic attraction toward a person. Can also be used as a verb, similarly to crush.

Relationships

  • Aromate-a platonic friend who’s pretty much your soulmate but in a friend way.

  • Queerplatonic / Quasiplatonic-describes a relationship, or the partner in such a relationship, that has the same sort of access, closeness, intimacy, etc, that is usually associated with a romantic relationship, but is not romantic. Quasiplatonic is used by people who are not comfortable with using the word queer. Often abbreviated as QP.

  • Soft romo-an adjective to describe something, like a relationship, as something that is low-level romantic. Soft-romo relationship is like a low-level romantic relationship. A soft-romo relationship is somewhere in between of a QPR and a romantic relationship.

Other Terms

  • Nonamory-not wanting to form a romantic relationship, regardless of romantic orientation

  • Romance Repulsion-a reaction to romance; someone who is romance repulsed is repulsed, “turned off”, disgusted, or otherwise uncomfortable with romance and/or romantic things.

  • Sex Repulsion-a reaction to sex; someone who is sex repulsed is repulsed, “turned off”, disgusted, or otherwise uncomfortable with sex and/or sexual things.

  • Touch aversion-similar to romance and sex repulsion, but describes being averse (repulse, intense dislike, discomfort, disgust) to being touched and/or touching others.
