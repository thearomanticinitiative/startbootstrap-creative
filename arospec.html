The Aromantic Spectrum

Gray Aromantic

Someone who experiences romantic attraction very rarely. Sometimes called grayromantic. Spelling of "gray" depends on location. Flag created by tumblr user lesbiangamora in this post.

Demiromantic

Someone who experiences romantic attraction only after forming a strong emotional connection with someone. It is unknown who originally created this flag.

Lithromantic/Akoiromantic

Someone who either feels romantic attraction but does not want it reciprocated, or someone who feels romantic attraction but has it fade when it is reciprocated.This flag was originally created by tumblr user thejasmineelf.

Quoiromantic/WTFromantic

Someone who has an inability or extreme difficulty telling the difference between romantic and platonic attraction; someone who feels categories of romantic attraction/platonic attraction don't make sense for them; someone who feels that categories such as romantic/platonic attraction are inacessible/nonsensical /inapplicable etc. to them. Prounounced kwa-romantic. The flag was originally created by tumblr users flowercrownprincette and ragingqueermisandrist here.

Cupioromantic

Someone who feels little to no romantic attraction (is aromantic spectrum) who also desires romance and traditionally romantic things. The flag is credited to deactivated tumblr user primadolly.

Frayromantic

Someone who feels romantic attraction only to strangers, and has that attraction fade when they get to know the person. Sometimes referred to as the opposite of demiromantic. Flag created by tumblr and deviantart user pridearchive here.

Recipromantic

Someone who only feels romantic attraction after realizing another person is romantically attracted to them. Flag created by tumblr and deviantart user pridearchive here.

Apothiromantic

Someone who feels little to no romantic attraction (is aromantic spectrum) and also is romance repulsed. Can be described as the opposite of cupioromantic. Flag creator unknown.

Aroflux

Someone who's identity fluctuates between feeling and not feeling romantic attraction. Flag created by unknown.

Klaperomantic

Someone who feels as though their ability (or potential) to experience traditional romantic attraction has been taken away from them due to abuse, trauma, neurodivergency, illness, or any other similar factor, including any combination of them. Note that this identity is for people who are neurodivergent, trauma survivors, etc. Flag created by tumblr user aroacethings here.

Requiesromantic

A label for individuals who experience limited attraction due to being drained of energy for any variety of reasons. Comes from the Latin root for ‘rest’. Emotional exhaustion may be the result of previous romantic endeavours and past experiences dealing with romance, or from something else just as emotionally draining. Flag creator unknown.

Arospike

Someone who usually does not feel romantic attraction but occasionally has it rapidly skyrocket into intense attraction, then plummet down to aromanticism again. Flag created by tumblr user pride-flags-for-us here.

What's the Difference Between Lithromantic and Akoiromantic?

Lithromantic and Akoiromantic are two terms that mean the same thing. However, lithromantic is older and was the original term. The prefix lith- means stone, and the term was coined by the tumblr blog lithromantic. An anonymous user asked why they chose the prefix stone for their romantic orientation, which is a fair question. The following is an excerpt from lithromantic’s reply: “We looked at other terms for romantic and sexual orientations, and we noticed that most of them relied of latin or greek roots. So we went looking for a root which meant something at all like “experiences without desire to reciprocate”. We didn’t find anything. I mean, anything close, not anything we could figure out how to twist around and make it mean what we wanted. But, we’re both AFAB queers, one of us Stone, so we were familiar with Stone Butch sexuality. For those of you who don’t know, Butch/Femme is sort of the Old Guard of lesbian sexuality (not to say butch/femme isn’t still a thing, but a particular type of butche/femme was historically the norm for lesbians), and Stone is a particular type of sexual expression. Someone who is Stone likes to provide sexual stimulation to a partner, but does not like to receive sexual stimulation themself. This was the closest allegory that we could find, and we chose to call this in our naming of this romantic orientation.”

However, controversy soon arose about the term. Some people were concerned that the term was appropriation, and decided to make an alternative. The most commonly used alternatives were akoiromantic and akoineromantic (not in common use anymore). An anonymous user asked pride-flags-for-us, “Do you happen to know an alternative to lith or am I fine to use it (i'm a transboy with demiboy leanings)? It's really the only sexuality that described my sexuality the best. But I've heard asexual is similar..” and they replied, "The prefix lith- is appropriative of lesbian culture which is why the lith- communities are working towards creating a different prefix. So far akoine- is the most accepted one. Anyone can use the akoine- label as long as it fits your sexuality/romantic”. Another post about it commented on it as neutrally as possible: “Some people take problem with lithromantic as it comes from stone butch terminology, which is appropriated from the lesbian community. I’m not making any definitive statements about the issue currently as I haven’t seen any actual lesbian-identified people speaking out about this, and most commentary I have seen comes from people outside the lesbian community saying it’s appropriation. However, that doesn’t mean it’s not appropriation. I’m not making any definitive statements about it either way at the moment, but since it’s still a debatable issue, it might be best to avoid the term and use akoiromantic instead.” Akoiromantic emerged from this discussion as an alternative to lithromantic.

Many lesbians argued against this being appropriation, however, and some spoke out against using akoiromantic. The following are excerpts from tumblr user ladyxaxa’s argument: “Hey! Stone femme (yes, it’s a thing) lithromantic queer woman here. I’ve been tracing this issue for a while, and I agree that most of the people saying it’s appropriation aren’t actually lesbian or stone identified. Meanwhile, the creator of the term (who does identify as stone/lithsexual) has explicitly said that it’s not appropriation, and I and my homoromantic asexual QPP agree. In fact, I would argue that not calling it lithromantic is erasure. . . . Basically, what I’m trying to say that by not calling it lith, it erases the history that the explanation of this experience comes from lesbian/stone activism. Ironically, by calling something appropriation that really, really isn’t because queer woman and stone people specifically intended it for sharing, the history of that sharing is erased. There are ‘actual lesbians’ (as many posts put it) who use this term and think it’s okay for you to use this term if you identify with it (and I’m obviously not saying you can’t use akoiromantic or another proposed term, although personally I think etymologically lith makes so much more sense specifically because it retains the link with stoneness). In other words, the concept that lith/akoiromantic indicates would not have been articulated when it was if not for lesbians and stone people and them relating their experiences to aromanticism, and I think it’s important to recognize that by retaining the words that we gave it.” So really, the only difference between akoiromantic and lithromantic is their roots, not the meaning. Currently, both are recognized in the aromantic community. (Note: the author is neither lith/akoiromantic nor lesbian. This is not meant to be an influential piece, but informative.)
