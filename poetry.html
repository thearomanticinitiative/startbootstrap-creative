I Keep It To Myself

fear -it’s a sin,

we don’t talk about it.

i don’t let it show:

how much i fear

everything,

everyone.

i keep my mouth

closed.

heart: closed.

eyes: closed.

i breathe

-softly, silently-

without words.

love -i don’t own it,

not a single piece of it.

as far as i can reach

i only feel

ashes

cold ashes-

the attempt to burn every desire.

words -wild things,

like beasts, they bite

at my fragile pride.

i do not sleep

in peace.

The Lake Part 1

I am what I am,

And you can try to change me,

And you can try to hold me,

But I am what I am,

And you cannot change me.

The moon, the stars, the trees,

Your face, your hopes, your dreams,

All are reflected in me,

But I twist and change them

Until they become my own.

On the surface you see these things,

These reflections,

But truly I am opaque.

You cannot see through me,

And you cannot see the true me.

You cannot roughly catch me

With a rope or net,

But maybe if you try gently,

You may hold me as your own

Or I’ll slip through the cracks.

I change my color each day,

Just as I change my mood.

And you may one day fear me,

The next entranced by my depths

Or yearn to find out more of what is hidden from you.

Another world lives within me,

At once so hard to see

Yet infinitely more real than the one

In which you reside,

At least it is that way to me.

You may pass me by,

Unavoidable yet unnoticed,

Or say you’ll come again,

When you do not have better things to do.

I don’t believe you when you say this,

For they never do.

Sticky Notes

I used to collect them.

Greens, blues, pinks, yellows

I wrote down small things on them,

They laid scattered across my desk,

Bunching on the floor.

Simple, pointless words, yet

I hoarded them.

To Make Orchards from a Graveyard

Forgiveness is radical,

But forgiveness is cruel.

Forgiveness is licking your wounds clean

And saying : look-

At least I have something to drink.

Forgiveness is saying : you hurt me,

But I have more scars

Self-inflicted,

Know their phantom promises

And have the heart to spare you.

If I hold on to this body

As a fury & god of vengeance

It will never be but a tomb.

I have to learn forgiveness

Of both you & me & my pain

If I want anything

To grow inside of me.

I forgive you ; it has nothing to do with you.

Watch me love myself so radically

It makes grown men cry.

Forgotten Fairytale

I’ll tell you a story of once upon a time,

When a fairytale princess her laws defied,

When her luck ran out and the tides turned,

She closed her eyes and burned.

When a fairytale prince travelled far and wide,

He searched for his lady but all he found,

Was ashes on the ground.

I’ll tell you a story of once upon a time,

When to a fairytale forest water was denied,

The leaves shrivelled and withered where they hung,

And a lament of life was sung.

I’ll tell you a story of once upon a time,

When a fairytale horse his master could not find,

He wandered through wasteland and never returned,

But centuries later they found his bones.

I’ll tell you a story of once upon a time,

When a fairytale queen watched her daughter die,

She lived on for years though it was death she craved,

And threw herself into a watery grave.

I’ll tell you a story of once upon a time,

When a fairytale witch felt pain and cried,

She fled to the forest from us to hide,

And her red blood flowed til the day she died.

I’ll tell you a story of once upon a time,

When a fairytale watcher felt so inclined,

To weave a tale where all was well,

But his words damned the witch to Hell.

I’ll tell you a story of once upon a time,

When a fairytale reader their laws defined,

And because they never knew to mourn,

Our happy ever after was born.
